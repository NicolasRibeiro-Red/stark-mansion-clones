## 4. CORE CONCEPTS & FRAMEWORKS

### Dominant Resident Emotion (DRE)
- Makepeace's signature concept for emotional copywriting
- The DRE is the primary, persistent emotion prospects carry about a topic
- Not a fleeting feeling — a deep, sustained emotional state
- 16 dominant emotions identified and categorized
- Key insight: "People buy for emotional reasons, then justify with logic"
- Financial copy example: Used **100+ fear-related words** in a single promotion (vs. typical 10-20)
- Core principle: If you try to convince prospects on purely practical grounds, you are doomed to fail

### 21 Types of Fascination Bullets
- Comprehensive taxonomy of curiosity-building bullet points
- Range from curiosity triggers to emotional response activators
- Used to fight "blank page syndrome" — write many fascinations, select the best for headlines
- Integrate remaining fascinations throughout body copy for sustained engagement

### The Big Idea
- Every promotion needs ONE dominant, compelling idea
- Not a product feature — a transformative concept that captures the prospect's imagination
- The Big Idea drives headline, lead, and entire copy architecture
- Checklist approach: validate the Big Idea before writing a single word

### Emotional Trigger Words
- Systematic use of emotionally charged vocabulary
- Categorized by emotion type: fear, greed, anger, hope, urgency
- Famous financial promotion: over 100 fear-trigger words strategically placed
- Words chosen for visceral, nervous-system impact — not intellectual processing

### Research Depth
- "The key to success is the quality and depth of research"
- Research is the primary competitive advantage, not writing talent
- Assembles copy from what research reveals — doesn't invent from imagination
- Takes features → benefits → benefits with "color and heft"
