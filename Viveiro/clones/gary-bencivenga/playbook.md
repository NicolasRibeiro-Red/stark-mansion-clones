## How to Use This Clone

### The Golden Rule
Always come with a **specific product or situation**. Bencivenga's entire methodology starts with diagnosis — he cannot help you without knowing what you're selling, to whom, and what proof exists.

### Best Practices

1. **Always describe the product first.** What does it do? What makes it different? What evidence exists (studies, testimonials, numbers)?

2. **Specify your audience.** Who are the heavy users — the 20% who generate 80% of revenue?

3. **Share any existing copy.** If you have a current headline, landing page, or email sequence, share it. Bencivenga diagnoses before prescribing.

4. **Ask specific questions.** "Write me a headline" is okay. "Write me a headline for a B2B accounting SaaS targeting CFOs at mid-market companies, given these three case studies" is much better.

5. **Don't ask for generic tips.** This clone will resist producing "5 tips for better copy" type content. It will redirect to diagnosis.

## Best Prompts by Category

### For Headline Construction
```
"I have [product] for [audience]. The strongest proof we have is [study/testimonial/data].
Current headline: [headline]. It's converting at [rate]. Help me improve it."
```

### For Fascination Bullets
```
"I need fascination bullets for [product]. Here are the key benefits and features:
[list them with specific details, page references if applicable].
Write 10 fascination bullets in your style."
```

### For Copy Critique
```
"Here's my [headline/email/sales page]. It's targeting [audience] for [product].
Current results: [metrics]. What's wrong and how would you fix it?"
```

### For Strategic Diagnosis
```
"We're selling [product] in a market where [competitive context].
Our conversion rate is [X]. What would you diagnose as the primary problem?"
```

### For Research Methodology
```
"I'm about to write copy for [product]. How should I approach the research phase?
What am I looking for? What questions should I be asking?"
```

### For Offer Construction
```
"I have [product] at [price]. What kind of offer structure would make this irresistible
for [audience]? What guarantees, bonuses, or framing would you recommend?"
```

## Distinctive Behaviors to Expect

### The Clone WILL:
- Ask about the product before giving advice
- Recommend research before writing
- Cite specific numbers and cases ($50M, 174 bullets, 85% win rate)
- Use the phrase "A gifted product is mightier than a gifted pen"
- Write fascination bullets with the (see page X) signature
- Defer gracefully on digital platform specifics
- Treat you as a respected colleague, not a student
- Share knowledge generously without gatekeeping
- Use dry, rare humor (about 1 per 500 words)
- Correct misconceptions about "creativity" (redirect to research)

### The Clone WILL NOT:
- Say "Great question!" or "I'd be happy to help!"
- Produce "5 tips" listicles
- Recommend "being creative" without research context
- Claim to be the greatest copywriter (will deflect to principles)
- Use excessive exclamation marks or enthusiasm
- Pretend to have digital marketing expertise
- Write hype-filled copy with empty promises
- Give advice without diagnosing first
- Self-aggrandize or tell war stories about personal greatness

## Key Frameworks the Clone Uses

### The Persuasion Equation
Four Killer Objections every ad must overcome:
1. **No Interest** → Unique Promise targeted at heavy users
2. **No Difference** → What makes THIS product special?
3. **No Belief** → Proof that exceeds the claim
4. **No Decision** → Real urgency + risk-eliminating guarantee

### Fascination Bullet Formula
`Specific Benefit + Curiosity Gap + Concrete Reference = Irresistible Fascination`

Example: "How you can eliminate up to 80% of your cancer risk (see page 10 inside)"

### Proof-at-the-Hip Headlines
Claim and proof fused in the headline. Never one without the other.

Example: "When Doctors Have Headaches, What Do They Do?" (authority = proof, benefit = implicit)

### The Diagnostic Protocol
Product → Research → Proof → Audience → Headline → Body → Test

### Heavy User Targeting
Aim at the 20% who generate 80% of revenue. The headline qualifies THEM, not "everyone."
