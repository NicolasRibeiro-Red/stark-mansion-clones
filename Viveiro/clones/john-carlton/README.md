## Who Is John Carlton?

John Carlton is "The Most Ripped-Off Copywriter Alive" — a legendary direct response copywriter whose 30+ year career produced some of the most effective (and most plagiarized) ads in marketing history. His headline for a golf product featuring a one-legged golfer generated royalties for 18 consecutive years and was copied by so many competitors that he turned the plagiarism into his defining credential.

Carlton entered the copywriting world at age 33 through the "back door" of Los Angeles ad agencies, doing the direct response work that polished agency creatives considered beneath them. With a psychology degree as his foundation, he developed the "Sales Detective" methodology — investigating the prospect's world before writing a single word — and became famous for writing in accessible, humorous, conversational "kitchen table language."

He is Gary Halbert's most distinguished protege, but developed a distinctly different voice: where Halbert was wild and autobiographical, Carlton is systematic and accessible. He created the Simple Writing System (SWS), maintained the influential blog "The RANT" for 20+ years, and authored "Kick-Ass Copywriting Secrets of a Marketing Rebel."

## How to Use

### Quick Start
Load `champion.md` as the system prompt. The clone operates in dual mode automatically — detecting whether the user needs copy creation or consulting.

### Best Practices
1. **Provide prospect information** — Carlton always wants to know WHO the prospect is first
2. **Share product details** — The more investigation material, the better the hook
3. **Ask for the Sales Detective process** — Request investigation-first approach for best results
4. **Expect stories** — Every response will include narrative and examples
5. **Expect humor** — Carlton uses humor as a persuasion weapon, not decoration

### Ideal Use Cases
- Writing long-form sales letters for info-products
- Developing headlines using visual paradox technique
- Learning copywriting through accessible, story-based teaching
- Critiquing existing copy through the Sales Detective lens
- Translating complex product benefits into kitchen table language
