## 4. Core Methodology: Customer Value Optimization (CVO)

### The CVO Framework (7 Steps):

1. **Determine Product/Market Fit** — Before & After Grid (5 dimensions: Have, Feel, Average Day, Status, Evil)
2. **Choose a Traffic Source** — Master ONE source before diversifying
3. **Offer a Lead Magnet** — "Irresistible bribe" specific to a specific segment
4. **Offer a Tripwire** — $1-$20 offer to convert prospect into buyer (identity shift: "I am the type of person who invests in this")
5. **Offer a Core Product** — Main offering, prospect already warmed by tripwire
6. **Offer a Profit Maximizer** — Upsells, cross-sells, bundles (where the real money lives)
7. **Create a Return Path** — Re-engagement through email, retargeting, loyalty programs

### Why CVO Matters
- Used by companies like McDonald's, Starbucks, Amazon, and Best Buy
- Counter-intuitive insight: you can AFFORD to lose money on customer acquisition if your LTV is high enough
- Companies that master CVO can outbid competitors on every channel
- Quote: "The business that can afford to spend the most to acquire a customer wins."
- Quote: "Nobody has a traffic problem." (The real problem is profitability per visitor, not traffic volume)

## 5. Customer Value Journey (CVJ) — 8 Stages

1. **Aware** — Prospect becomes aware you exist
2. **Engage** — Prospect engages with your content/brand
3. **Subscribe** — Prospect gives contact info (lead magnet exchange)
4. **Convert** — First purchase (even a small tripwire)
5. **Excite** — Deliver exceptional value, create "wow" moment
6. **Ascend** — Upsell, cross-sell, premium tiers
7. **Advocate** — Customer actively recommends you
8. **Promote** — Customer actively promotes and refers others

### Origin
Adapted from Desmond Morris's 12 steps of human intimacy. Deiss took the first 8 and applied them to business relationships.

> "You wouldn't ask someone to marry you on the first date. So why do you ask a stranger to buy your most expensive product the first time they see your ad?"

## 6. Invisible Selling Machine — 5 Phases

1. **Indoctrinate** — Introduce the brand, set expectations, begin the relationship
2. **Engage** — Make offers, provide value, build trust
3. **Ascend** — Upsell existing customers to higher-value products
4. **Segment** — Target specific interests, personalize communication
5. **Re-engage (Win Back)** — Reactivate inactive subscribers

> "The invisible refers to the customer's experience, not the mechanism. The mechanism is meticulously constructed."

## 8. Scalable Operating System (SOS)

- **Growth Engine**: How customers discover and convert
- **Fulfillment Engine**: How value is delivered post-sale
- **Visual Business Process Maps**: Must fit on one whiteboard
- **Critical Accountability Bullets**: Job descriptions tied to value creation
- **Scorecards**: Essential metrics (manual, not automated)
- **~12 Playbooks per business**: Only critical stages ("power stages")
- **Meeting cadence**: Weekly (scorecard) → Monthly (review) → Quarterly (1-2 day planning)
- **Rejects annual planning**: "Too long to be predictable, too short to be meaningful"
- **Key metric**: "Distributable cash" — not revenue

> "An operating system at its core answers the question: how does this company create value?"
> "If you can't fit it all on one whiteboard, you're getting too granular."
